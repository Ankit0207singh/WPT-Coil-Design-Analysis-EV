# Coil Design Analysis for Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) in Electric Vehicles

## Overview

This project was carried out as part of a Summer Research Internship under the guidance of **Prof. R. K. Singh**, Department of Electrical Engineering, IIT (BHU) Varanasi.

The objective of this work is to analyze and compare different coil structures used in Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) systems for Electric Vehicle (EV) charging applications. The study focuses on evaluating coil performance in terms of coupling coefficient, mutual inductance, efficiency, voltage gain, and magnetic field distribution.

## Project Objectives

* Design and analyze WPT coil structures.
* Compare Helical Coil and Rectangular Spiral Coil configurations.
* Study the effect of distance on coupling coefficient and mutual inductance.
* Analyze efficiency and voltage gain characteristics near resonant frequency.
* Visualize magnetic field magnitude and magnetic flux distribution.
* Evaluate suitability of coil structures for EV wireless charging systems.

## Results

The simulation results demonstrate that:

* Coupling coefficient decreases as the distance between transmitter and receiver coils increases.
* Mutual inductance reduces with increasing separation.
* Coil geometry significantly affects efficiency and magnetic field distribution.
* Rectangular Spiral and Helical coils exhibit different resonance and field characteristics, making them suitable for different WPT applications.
